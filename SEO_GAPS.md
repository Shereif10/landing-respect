# SEO_GAPS.md — RESPECT Landing Page

**Purpose:** Document every Landing Page section (per PRD.md §8, Information Architecture) that currently has no source content in SEO_Document.docx. This document identifies and characterizes gaps only. It does not propose copy, does not invent content, and does not resolve any gap — resolution is a separate, later step (CONTENT_MAP.md and beyond).

**Source reviewed:** SEO_Document.docx (approved as the single source of truth for content, superseding content.md).

---

## Gap 1 — Hero

- **Section name:** Hero (PRD §8, IA #1)
- **Why it is missing:** SEO_Document.docx contains no dedicated landing-page-level Hero. The only Hero content present is 10 separate per-service Hero paragraphs, each tied to a specific `/services/*` website page — none of them is framed as a landing-page-wide, company-level Hero.
- **Derivable from existing SEO content:** No. A landing-page Hero needs to speak to the business as a whole, not to one service; none of the 10 service Heroes is written for that purpose.
- **Requires business input:** Yes — the core landing-page positioning/message for the Hero has not been defined anywhere.
- **Requires the SEO specialist:** Undetermined — depends on whether the landing page will carry any organic/indexable weight. If it is purely paid-traffic and non-indexed, this is primarily a conversion-copy decision; if it is expected to rank, SEO input on Hero keyword framing would apply. This distinction has not been confirmed.

## Gap 3 — How We Help Businesses Grow (Partial)

- **Section name:** How We Help Businesses Grow (PRD §8, IA #4)
- **Why it is missing/partial:** A single short services-intro line exists (in both languages) under what the document labels the "Services" heading. The 10 full service pages exist as source material, but each is a deep, independent page (Meta block, H1, Hero, Problem, Methodology, Deliverables, FAQ, CTA) — not condensed into equal, brief representations suited to one landing-page section.
- **Derivable from existing SEO content:** Partially — service names and one-line focus areas could theoretically be drawn from each service's Meta Title/Focus Keyword, but condensing 10 deep pages into one section is an editorial decision, not extraction.
- **Requires business input:** Yes — whether to present all 10 services or a curated subset, and at what depth, has not been decided.
- **Requires the SEO specialist:** Likely — condensing existing keyword-bearing service copy while preserving intent is the kind of task suited to whoever maintains the SEO document.
